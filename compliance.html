Compliance

Effective Date: March 11, 2026
Last Updated: March 11, 2026

Tomb of Light is being developed with a privacy-first and responsibility-based approach to digital legacy preservation. Because the platform is intended to involve family history, portraits, lineage records, and other potentially sensitive materials, compliance is a core part of how we design policies, workflows, and future platform systems.

This page summarizes our current compliance direction and the standards we aim to follow as the Tomb of Light platform grows.

1. Privacy and Data Protection

Tomb of Light aims to protect personal information and family legacy content through privacy-conscious design, controlled data handling, and restricted operational access. We are committed to minimizing unnecessary exposure of personal information and maintaining a structure that separates sensitive private data from any future public ownership layers.

Additional details about personal data handling are available in our Privacy Policy.

2. Consent and Authority Requirements

Users are responsible for ensuring they have the necessary authority, permission, or legal basis before submitting personal information, portraits, lineage details, or family records involving other living individuals.

Where family submissions involve spouses, children, relatives, or other living persons, users should not provide that information unless they have proper consent or another lawful basis to do so.

Minor-Related Information

If a submission includes information relating to a minor, the submitting user must have appropriate parental or guardian authority to provide that information. Sensitive information regarding minors should be handled with elevated caution.

3. Off-Chain Treatment of Sensitive Information

Tomb of Light is being developed with the intention that sensitive family information, legacy narratives, personal records, and identifying materials are not written directly to public blockchain systems. Where future blockchain-backed ownership features are used, they are intended to reference ownership or platform relationships without publicly exposing private family data.

This architectural separation is an important compliance principle for the platform.

4. User Responsibilities

Users are expected to:

  • Provide accurate information when submitting requests, content, or account details
  • Submit only content they are authorized to share
  • Respect the privacy, dignity, and legal rights of other individuals
  • Refrain from uploading unlawful, deceptive, abusive, or infringing materials
  • Use the platform in a way that is consistent with applicable laws and platform policies

5. Regulatory Awareness

As Tomb of Light grows, we intend to continue evaluating applicable privacy, consumer protection, data governance, digital asset, and online service requirements that may apply to our operations depending on where users are located and what platform features are active.

This may include review of state privacy laws, data rights frameworks, record-handling obligations, contract standards, and future digital asset compliance considerations.

6. Security and Operational Controls

Compliance depends in part on sound security and operational discipline. Tomb of Light uses and plans to continue improving measures such as encrypted connections, controlled access practices, monitoring, and internal review of privacy and security risks.

More details are available on our Security page.

7. Data Rights and Requests

Depending on applicable law, users may have rights to request access to, correction of, deletion of, or information about certain personal data. Tomb of Light provides a Data Request page for submitting applicable requests.

Additional notices for certain jurisdictions may be posted on our State Privacy Notices page.

8. Platform Development and Evolving Standards

Tomb of Light is an evolving platform. Our compliance practices, review procedures, consent workflows, and technical controls may change over time as we expand features, improve governance, and respond to legal or operational developments.

We may update this page as our systems mature or as new compliance obligations become relevant.

9. No Guarantee of Universal Availability

Certain future platform features, including blockchain-backed ownership functions, account features, or advanced lineage services, may not be available in all jurisdictions or for all users. Availability may depend on legal, technical, operational, or eligibility considerations.

10. Related Policies

Please also review our Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Security pages for related standards and requirements.
