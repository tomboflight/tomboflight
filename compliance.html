Governance Framework

Compliance & Legal

Tomb of Light’s public-facing framework is designed to support privacy, respectful family participation, user rights, accessibility, and responsible stewardship as the platform evolves.

Request Access Terms of Service
Core Principles
1

Privacy and dignity

Family legacy systems should be handled with care, especially when records involve living relatives, sensitive stories, or private identifying details.

2

Consent and participation

Family participation should not be assumed casually. Depending on the context, consent, authorization, or lawful basis may be needed before including living individuals.

3

Rights and accountability

Users should be able to review key policies, request help, and use available channels for privacy choices, data requests, corrections, and support.

Privacy and Data Handling

Private family material should be handled in controlled systems.

Tomb of Light is designed around the principle that sensitive family content, personal stories, and related records should be treated carefully and should not be openly published merely for convenience or visibility. The platform direction favors protected systems over public exposure.

  • Private family data should be handled with restraint.
  • Public-facing ownership layers should avoid unnecessary disclosure.
  • Published policy pages help communicate expected handling standards.
Family Participation Standards

Living individuals

Where family experiences involve living people, inclusion should be respectful, accurate, and supported by appropriate authority, consent, or legitimate basis depending on the context.

  • Accuracy matters
  • Correction and review pathways matter
  • Casual misuse should be discouraged

Minors and sensitive family information

If minors or highly sensitive family data are ever involved, parent or guardian permission and elevated protection expectations should apply.

  • Parent or guardian authorization
  • Private-by-default handling
  • Reduced public exposure expectations

Evolving Legal and Regulatory Context

This framework may evolve as the platform grows.

Digital lineage systems, privacy expectations, consumer rights, and blockchain-related legal considerations can change over time. Tomb of Light may revise public-facing documents, internal practices, and governance standards as the product matures.

  • Policies may be updated when operations change.
  • Compliance expectations may vary by jurisdiction and product scope.
  • Additional safeguards may be introduced as access expands.
Important Limitation

Public website materials are informational and operational in nature.

Tomb of Light’s public-facing pages are intended to explain the platform direction, usage expectations, and trust framework. They do not replace formal legal advice for users or visitors. Where needed, individuals should consult their own legal or professional advisors.
