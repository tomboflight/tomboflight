Core Principles

The framework begins with three public-facing commitments.

Tomb of Light is shaped around the idea that digital lineage systems should be governed with care, clarity, and respect for the people represented inside them.

01

Privacy and Dignity

Family legacy records should be handled with restraint, especially when they involve living relatives, sensitive history, or identifying details.

02

Consent and Participation

Family participation should not be assumed casually. Depending on the context, authorization, consent, or another lawful basis may be needed.

03

Rights and Accountability

Users should be able to review policies, request help, and use clear channels for privacy choices, corrections, data requests, and support.

Privacy and Data Handling

Private family material should be handled in controlled systems.

Tomb of Light is built around the principle that sensitive family content, stories, lineage details, and related materials should be treated carefully and not exposed casually for convenience or visibility.

  • Private family data should be handled with restraint.
  • Public-facing layers should avoid unnecessary disclosure of sensitive details.
  • Published policy pages help communicate expected standards of handling.
Family Participation Standards

Respectful inclusion matters when real families are involved.

Compliance is not only about documents. It also depends on how people are represented, what authority exists for participation, and how sensitive relationships are handled.

Living Individuals

Where a family experience involves living people, inclusion should be respectful, accurate, and supported by appropriate authority, consent, or legitimate basis.

  • Accuracy matters
  • Correction and review pathways matter
  • Casual misuse should be discouraged

Minors and Sensitive Family Information

If minors or highly sensitive family materials are ever involved, parent or guardian permission and heightened protection expectations should apply.

  • Parent or guardian authorization
  • Private-by-default handling
  • Reduced public exposure expectations

Disputes and Corrections

Where information is inaccurate, disputed, or incomplete, the platform should support a responsible path for correction, review, and respectful follow-up.

  • Correction-aware handling
  • Review pathways
  • Integrity-focused stewardship

Evolving Legal and Regulatory Context

This framework may evolve as the platform grows.

Digital lineage systems, privacy expectations, consumer rights, and platform governance considerations can change over time. Tomb of Light may revise public-facing documents, internal practices, and governance standards as the product matures.

  • Policies may be updated when operations change.
  • Compliance expectations may vary by jurisdiction and product scope.
  • Additional safeguards may be introduced as access expands.
Important Limitation

Public website materials are informational and operational in nature.

Tomb of Light’s public-facing pages are intended to explain platform direction, usage expectations, and the trust framework surrounding the service. They do not replace formal legal advice for visitors or users. Where needed, individuals should consult their own legal or professional advisors.
