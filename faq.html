Frequently asked questions

FAQ

These answers explain Tomb of Light’s current public position on privacy, ownership, family participation, policy expectations, and future platform direction.

What is Tomb of Light?

Tomb of Light is being built as a secure, privacy-first family legacy platform. The long-term goal is to help families preserve portraits, stories, relationships, and generational memory in protected systems while enabling future ownership structures that do not expose private family data to the public blockchain.

Is Tomb of Light a public social media platform?

No. The platform direction is not centered on public posting or public social sharing. It is centered on controlled legacy preservation, protected access, and respectful family participation.

Will private family data be placed on-chain?

The platform direction is to keep private family data, stories, media, and sensitive records off-chain. If an NFT or blockchain ownership layer is used, it should represent ownership or reference status only and should avoid revealing sensitive family details.

What does the NFT represent?

A future NFT layer is intended to represent ownership or reference rights tied to a protected family vault or legacy experience. It is not intended to serve as a public dump of family records or private personal data.

Can I add living family members without their knowledge?

Tomb of Light’s public-facing policy direction is that living individuals should not be included casually or without appropriate authority, permission, or lawful basis where required. Family participation should be handled carefully and respectfully.

What about children or minors?

If minors are ever included in a family experience, parental or guardian permission should be required. Sensitive family information involving minors should receive heightened protection and private-by-default treatment.

Is Tomb of Light live as a full vault and NFT platform yet?

Public-facing website materials may describe the platform vision and planned direction, but some features may still be under development or not yet fully launched. Pages like Sign In or Sign Up may currently act as front-end shells until the full backend systems are connected.

How is the platform planning to protect family content?

The intended protection model includes controlled storage, privacy-first design, access controls, policy-based handling, and future security improvements for encrypted and permission-sensitive family materials.

Can I request correction or removal of my information?

Yes. If you believe information is inaccurate, should be corrected, or should be removed, use the Data Request page or contact Tomb of Light through the listed policy contact channels.

Does Tomb of Light sell personal information?

The site’s current policy direction states that Tomb of Light does not sell personal information in the traditional sense and does not use optional analytics cookies unless the user permits them where applicable.

How do cookies work on the website?

The site uses essential technologies for core functionality and may offer optional analytics controls. You can review or change those preferences through the Privacy Choices page.

What if a family member disputes inclusion in a legacy project?

Disputes should be handled through correction, review, or removal pathways. Because family identity can be sensitive, Tomb of Light’s policy direction supports respectful review rather than careless permanence.

Can Tomb of Light support inheritance or future transfer?

Future versions of the platform may support transfer, succession, inheritance, or role-based access workflows, but those systems should be implemented carefully and without compromising private family materials.

Where should I read more about privacy and legal terms?

Review the Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Cookie Policy, Security page, Compliance & Legal page, and Data Request page for the current public-facing framework.

Need more than a quick answer?

Review the core trust and policy pages to understand how Tomb of Light approaches privacy, security, family participation, and rights requests.
