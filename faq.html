Platform Status

Is Tomb of Light live?

The public website is live. Tomb of Light’s broader platform capabilities are being developed in stages. Public pages may describe the platform direction, trust framework, and prototype experience while operational features continue to evolve.

What can I do today?

Visitors can explore the public site, review trust pages, and request access to learn more about platform availability, onboarding, and future participation opportunities.

Product Concept

What is Tomb of Light?

Tomb of Light is being developed as a private digital legacy platform for preserving and exploring family lineage through structured visual experiences, protected records, and future ownership-linked systems.

Is this just an NFT project?

No. The broader concept centers on private family legacy, cinematic lineage exploration, and long-term stewardship. Any blockchain-related ownership layer is intended to support the platform without exposing private family content publicly.

Privacy and Blockchain

Will private family data be on-chain?

The intended direction is that private family stories, media, and sensitive records remain off-chain in controlled systems. Public blockchain components, if used, should avoid exposing unnecessary personal family information.

Why not put everything on the blockchain?

Public blockchains are not appropriate for unrestricted publication of sensitive family content. Tomb of Light is designed around dignity, privacy, and controlled stewardship, not radical public exposure.

Family Participation

Can I include living family members?

Family participation involving living individuals should be handled respectfully and, where appropriate, with consent, authorization, or another lawful basis depending on the facts.

What about children or minors?

If minors are ever included in a family experience, parent or guardian permission should be required, and heightened privacy protections should apply.

Access and Accounts

Can I create an account right now?

Access may be staged or limited depending on current product readiness. The public request-access flow is intended to help coordinate interest, onboarding, and future participation.

Why does the site use “Request Access”?

That language reflects a more controlled rollout and a premium onboarding direction. It helps align public interest with platform readiness rather than implying unrestricted mass access before the experience is fully matured.

Policies and Support

Where can I review your policies?

The website includes public pages for privacy, terms, cookie handling, accessibility, security, privacy choices, data requests, and state privacy notices.

How do I make a privacy or data request?

Use the public Data Requests page or other listed support or compliance contact channels to submit a request for review.
