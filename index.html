What Tomb of Light is building

A digital family vault, not just a profile page.

Tomb of Light is designed to help families preserve identity, memory, and lineage in a way that feels premium, secure, and future-ready.

Private family vaults

Family stories, portraits, records, and historical context are intended to live in protected storage, not in public blockchain metadata.

  • Controlled family access
  • Structured legacy records
  • Privacy-first architecture

Blockchain-backed ownership

The ownership layer is designed to anchor a family vault without placing sensitive personal details directly on-chain.

  • Non-identifying NFT references
  • Future transfer and inheritance workflows
  • Direct platform ownership experience

Immersive lineage experience

Tomb of Light is evolving toward an interactive, cinematic family tree experience that allows users to move through generations with meaning.

  • Portrait-centered storytelling
  • Generational navigation
  • Future immersive viewer expansion
How the platform works

Built to respect privacy, consent, and legacy.

The platform is designed so that ownership, memory, and family access can coexist without exposing private family records to the public internet.

1. Create a legacy profile

Start with a family anchor portrait and core lineage information. Add approved family members, stories, and memorial context over time.

2. Secure the vault

Family content is meant to remain protected in controlled storage with access permissions, consent rules, and future security monitoring.

3. Anchor ownership

When the ownership layer is active, a family vault can be linked to an NFT record that represents ownership without publishing sensitive family data.

Privacy and security come first.

Tomb of Light is building for families, not speculation. Our public-facing policies are designed to make clear what stays private, what may be anchored on-chain, and how consent matters.

Trust pillars

Why families and partners should take this seriously.

Consent-based family participation

Living individuals should not be added to a family experience without proper permission. Where minors are involved, parental or guardian consent is required.

  • Consent-aware onboarding direction
  • Removal and correction pathways
  • Identity and family sensitivity built into policy design

Private-by-default data treatment

Sensitive family media, names, stories, and records should remain off-chain and under controlled platform management rather than becoming publicly visible forever.

  • Off-chain protected storage model
  • Limited public blockchain exposure
  • Clear separation of ownership and family content
