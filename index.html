Platform foundation

Built like secure infrastructure, not just a gallery page.

Tomb of Light is being designed as a structured legacy system with security, consent, and private data separation at the center of the product.

Private legacy vaults

Family stories, portraits, and lineage records are intended to remain protected in controlled storage rather than being exposed publicly.

Identity and access control

Access to protected records is designed around controlled permissions, secure account workflows, and privacy-aware participation.

Off-chain sensitive records

Sensitive personal family information is not intended to be written directly to public blockchain networks.

Ownership-ready architecture

Tomb of Light is being developed with future ownership systems in mind while keeping private content separate from public references.

How the platform is structured

A technical foundation for digital family legacy.

The product direction combines secure data handling, premium presentation, and future-ready ownership architecture in one platform.

1. Family record layer

Portraits, stories, lineage data, and submitted records are intended to live in protected, platform-managed environments.

2. Permission layer

Living individuals, minors, and submitted family information should be handled under consent, authority, and access-control principles.

3. Ownership layer

Future blockchain-backed ownership features are intended to reference ownership without publishing sensitive family content on-chain.
