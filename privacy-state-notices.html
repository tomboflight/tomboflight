Additional Privacy Rights

State Privacy Notices

Residents of certain U.S. states may have additional privacy rights depending on applicable law. This page provides supplemental information about those rights and how Tomb of Light may process related requests where applicable.

Effective date: March 26, 2026.

Applicability

This page applies only where state privacy law grants rights to you.

Not every state law applies to every user or every interaction. Availability of rights depends on factors such as the applicable law, the nature of the relationship with Tomb of Light, the information involved, and whether legal exemptions or thresholds apply.

Categories of Information

Tomb of Light may collect different categories of information depending on the workflow involved.

  • Identifiers and contact information
  • Account credentials and related login information
  • Family member, relationship, and household information
  • Uploaded portraits, images, records, and supporting documents
  • Service, order, and support communications
  • Device, log, cookie, session, and security-related information
Purposes for Collection

Information may be collected and used to operate the service, fulfill requests, and protect the platform.

  • Create and manage accounts
  • Operate intake, family-record, and upload workflows
  • Support customer service, fulfillment, and communications
  • Maintain security, investigate misuse, and prevent fraud
  • Comply with legal, tax, audit, and governance obligations
Possible Rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have one or more of the following rights.

Access and Portability

  • Request access to certain personal information
  • Request information about categories of data collected
  • Request a portable copy where applicable

Correction and Deletion

  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion in certain circumstances
  • Understand that some records may be retained where legally permitted or required

Additional Rights

  • Appeal or reconsideration rights where required by law
  • Authorized agent rights where recognized by law
  • Additional disclosures about privacy practices where required
How to Exercise Rights

Submit requests through the Data Requests page or another listed channel.

Where applicable, Tomb of Light may honor qualifying requests submitted through the Data Requests page or another listed contact channel, subject to verification, security needs, and legal limitations.

Submit a Request
Authorized Agents

Authorized agents may be required to provide proof of authority.

If state law permits you to use an authorized agent, Tomb of Light may require documentation reasonably necessary to confirm both the identity of the requestor and the authority of the agent to act on the requestor’s behalf.

Verification and Limits

Tomb of Light may request information reasonably necessary to verify identity or authority.

Tomb of Light may request information reasonably necessary to verify identity, authority, or the scope of a request before responding. Certain information may be retained or withheld where permitted or required for security, fraud prevention, compliance, legal defense, tax, audit, or operational continuity reasons.

Sale or Sharing Statements

Additional notices may be provided if required by a particular law or data use.

Tomb of Light does not describe the public website as selling personal information for money in the ordinary sense. If a particular state law, analytics practice, or platform workflow requires a more specific notice, Tomb of Light may provide that notice separately.

Non-Discrimination

Tomb of Light will not unlawfully discriminate for exercising applicable privacy rights.

Where state privacy law applies, Tomb of Light will not unlawfully discriminate against you for exercising qualifying privacy rights, subject to lawful service limitations and the practical need to verify and process requests.

Updates

These notices may change as laws or platform practices evolve.

Tomb of Light may update these state privacy notices as state privacy laws, platform practices, interpretations, or legal requirements evolve.
