Privacy Policy

Privacy is part of how Tomb of Light protects family dignity.

This Privacy Policy explains how Tomb of Light may collect, use, protect, retain, and manage information through its public website, request flows, and related communications.

1. Scope

This policy applies to the public website and related interactions.

This policy applies to information collected through the Tomb of Light website, website forms, request-access workflows, support communications, privacy requests, and related compliance interactions unless a different notice is provided for a specific service, program, or offering.

2. Information We May Collect

Information may come from what you provide and from how the website operates.

Information You Provide

  • Name, email address, and contact details
  • Messages submitted through website forms
  • Request-access or support inquiry information
  • Privacy, compliance, or data request details
  • Any other information you choose to provide voluntarily

Information Collected Automatically

  • Basic browser and device information
  • IP address or approximate location signals
  • Website usage and interaction data
  • Cookie or similar preference information where used
  • Diagnostic and security-related technical signals

Why This Matters

Tomb of Light treats privacy as part of trust. Information is meant to support website operations, inquiries, user choices, and lawful business stewardship rather than careless exposure.

3. How Information May Be Used

Collected information may be used to operate, protect, and improve the website.

Tomb of Light may use collected information to operate the website, respond to inquiries, evaluate request-access interest, improve the user experience, maintain security, detect misuse, and support legal, compliance, or operational needs.

  • Provide and improve the public website
  • Respond to support, privacy, or compliance requests
  • Review onboarding or access-related interest
  • Maintain security and detect misuse
  • Meet legal, operational, or governance obligations
4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Essential site technologies may be used, and optional tools may be used where permitted.

Tomb of Light may use essential website technologies to support site functionality and may use optional analytics or related tools where permitted. Preference choices may be stored on the user’s device through browser-based mechanisms or similar technologies.

5. Sharing of Information

Information may be shared only where reasonably needed for operations, protection, or legal obligations.

Tomb of Light does not describe the public website as a system for selling personal information in the ordinary sense. Information may be shared with service providers, infrastructure providers, security providers, professional advisors, or authorities where reasonably needed for business operations, protection, compliance, or lawful obligations.

  • Website hosting and infrastructure support
  • Security and fraud-prevention services
  • Professional advisors and lawful requests
  • Business protection and operational continuity needs
6. Data Retention

Information may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary.

Information may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to operate the website, respond to requests, maintain records, protect the business, support continuity, and comply with applicable legal or operational obligations.

7. User Rights and Requests

Users may have rights depending on location and applicable law.

Depending on your location and the laws that apply, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, or additional information about how your data is handled.

Possible Rights

  • Access to certain personal information
  • Correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion in certain circumstances
  • Information about privacy practices

How to Submit a Request

8. Security

Reasonable safeguards are used, but no system can promise perfect security.

Tomb of Light uses and intends to continue improving reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect information. No method of transmission, storage, or internet-based handling can be guaranteed as absolutely secure.

9. Children and Minors

The public website is not intended to encourage unsupervised submissions by children.

The public website is not intended to encourage unsupervised submission of personal information by children. If Tomb of Light later supports family experiences involving minors, heightened authorization and privacy expectations should apply.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated as the platform evolves.

Tomb of Light may update this Privacy Policy as website features, operational practices, compliance expectations, or legal requirements change. Updated versions will be posted on this page with a revised effective date when appropriate.
