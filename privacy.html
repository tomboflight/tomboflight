Privacy Policy

Privacy is part of how Tomb of Light protects family dignity, private records, and controlled access.

This Privacy Policy explains how Tomb of Light may collect, use, protect, retain, disclose, and manage information through its public website, customer accounts, intake workflows, upload tools, support channels, and related platform operations.

Effective Date

This notice applies to Tomb of Light’s website and platform-facing workflows.

Effective date: March 26, 2026.

This policy applies to information collected through the Tomb of Light website, account registration, sign-in experiences, customer onboarding, intake steps, family mapping workflows, upload tools, support communications, privacy requests, order-related communications, and related compliance or operational interactions unless a different notice is provided for a specific product, feature, or program.

1. Information We May Collect

Information may come from what you provide, what you upload, and how the website or platform operates.

Account and Contact Information

  • Name, email address, and contact details
  • Account credentials and related authentication information
  • Profile, support, or onboarding communications
  • Request-access, sign-up, or service inquiry details

Family, Project, and Narrative Information

  • Family member names, relationships, and household details
  • Family history, narrative notes, lineage context, and project details
  • Customer-provided descriptions, preferences, and review feedback
  • Information entered into family graphs, intake forms, or related tools

Uploads and Verification Materials

  • Portraits, photos, and other image files
  • Birth, marriage, adoption, death, obituary, or supporting family records
  • Verification-related materials and supporting documents
  • Metadata reasonably associated with submitted files

Transaction and Service Information

  • Order details, billing-related records, and service history
  • Subscription or maintenance-plan information where offered
  • Project status, fulfillment communications, and review history

Automatically Collected Information

  • Basic browser, device, and operating environment details
  • IP address, approximate location signals, and diagnostics
  • Website or platform usage, log, and interaction data
  • Cookie, session, or preference information where used
  • Security-related technical events and abuse-prevention signals

Information From Others

  • Information provided by an authorized family representative
  • Materials submitted by a parent or legal guardian
  • Information from service providers that help operate the platform
2. Sources of Information

Information may be collected directly, automatically, or through authorized workflow participants.

  • Directly from you when you create an account, contact Tomb of Light, or submit forms
  • From files, records, and images you upload or otherwise provide
  • From your interactions with the website, platform, emails, and support channels
  • From family representatives, parents, guardians, or other persons you authorize
  • From service providers that support hosting, infrastructure, payments, security, or communications
3. How Information May Be Used

Collected information may be used to operate the platform, fulfill services, protect users, and support lawful business needs.

  • Create and manage customer accounts
  • Operate onboarding, intake, review, and family-record workflows
  • Receive, store, review, and organize uploaded materials
  • Support custom project creation, fulfillment, delivery, and maintenance services
  • Respond to support, privacy, compliance, or service requests
  • Maintain security, detect misuse, investigate fraud, and protect platform integrity
  • Comply with legal, tax, contractual, governance, or operational obligations
  • Improve website and platform experience, usability, and reliability
4. Sensitive and Verification-Related Materials

Users should submit only information reasonably necessary for the stated purpose.

Some uploads may contain highly personal, family, or identity-related information. Tomb of Light asks users to submit only the information reasonably necessary for the stated review or service purpose and not to include payment card information, Social Security numbers, or other unnecessary sensitive information unless Tomb of Light expressly requests it for a specific workflow.

Uploading verification-related materials does not guarantee approval, acceptance, legal validation, or platform publication. Tomb of Light may review submitted materials for workflow management, support, fraud prevention, security, compliance, and service fulfillment.

5. How Information May Be Shared

Information may be shared only where reasonably necessary for operations, protection, or legal obligations.

Tomb of Light does not describe the public website or platform as a system for selling personal information for money in the ordinary sense. Information may be disclosed to service providers, infrastructure providers, storage providers, payment processors, security providers, professional advisors, or authorities where reasonably needed for business operations, fraud prevention, compliance, legal obligations, or protection of users and the business.

  • Hosting, storage, and infrastructure support
  • Authentication, security, and fraud-prevention support
  • Payment, order, and operational service providers
  • Professional advisors such as lawyers, accountants, or auditors
  • Authorities or counterparties where disclosure is lawfully required or reasonably necessary
6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Essential site technologies may be used, and optional tools may be used where permitted.

Tomb of Light may use essential website or platform technologies to support security, sign-in, session integrity, preferences, and core functionality. Optional analytics or similar tools may be used where permitted. Preference choices may be stored on the user’s device through browser-based mechanisms or similar technologies.

7. Retention

Information may be kept for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.

Tomb of Light may retain information for different periods depending on the category of information, the service involved, the nature of the request, security needs, recordkeeping, fraud prevention, dispute resolution, and legal obligations.

Examples of Retention Criteria

  • How long an account remains active
  • Whether a project, order, or support matter is ongoing
  • Whether a record is needed for security, tax, audit, or legal reasons
  • Whether deletion would impair fraud prevention, dispute handling, or compliance duties

Category Examples

  • Account records may be kept while the account is active and for a reasonable period afterward
  • Uploads may be retained for review, fulfillment, support, fraud prevention, and legal defense needs
  • Support, privacy, and compliance records may be retained to document request handling
  • Logs and security-related records may be retained to maintain platform integrity and investigate misuse
8. User Rights and Requests

Users may have rights depending on location and applicable law.

Depending on your location and the laws that apply, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, portability, or additional information about how your information is handled. Some requests may require Tomb of Light to verify identity or authority before responding.

Possible Rights

  • Access to certain personal information
  • Correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion in certain circumstances
  • Information about privacy practices
  • Appeal or reconsideration rights where required by law

How to Submit a Request

Use the Data Requests page or another listed support or compliance channel to submit a request for review.

Verification

Tomb of Light may request information reasonably necessary to verify the identity of the requestor, the authority of an authorized agent, or the scope of the request.

9. Children and Minors

Special care is required when a submission involves a minor.

The public website is not directed to children under 13 for unsupervised use. If a submission, family record, or upload involves a minor, Tomb of Light expects that the submission is made by a parent, legal guardian, or other person with lawful authority to act on the minor’s behalf where required.

10. Security

Reasonable safeguards are used, but no system can promise perfect security.

Tomb of Light uses administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to reduce risk and protect information. No method of transmission, storage, or internet-based handling can be guaranteed as absolutely secure.
