Platform Overview

How Tomb of Light works

Tomb of Light is being developed as a secure family legacy platform where portraits, stories, and lineage records can be preserved in protected systems and later connected to ownership layers without making private family material public.

Core Process
1

Start with a family anchor

A family experience begins with a primary portrait, memorial point, or lineage anchor. This becomes the starting point for organizing the broader family legacy.

2

Build the protected vault

Stories, media, names, dates, and legacy records are intended to be managed in controlled systems rather than exposed in public blockchain metadata.

3

Enable future ownership

Once ownership features are activated, a family vault can be linked to a blockchain record that represents ownership while sensitive family material remains off-chain.

The Foundation

Private family content stays separate from public ownership records.

Tomb of Light is not designed to publish a family’s private memories to the blockchain. The platform direction is to keep personal family data, stories, portraits, and records protected in controlled storage while using blockchain only for limited ownership or reference purposes where appropriate.

  • Private family stories and records are intended to remain off-chain.
  • Ownership references should avoid exposing sensitive identifying details.
  • Permissions and consent matter before living family members are included.

What Makes This Different

A family legacy system, not a public social feed.

Tomb of Light is being shaped around long-term family continuity, controlled sharing, and secure digital preservation. The goal is not to create another public posting platform. The goal is to create a secure environment where family meaning can be preserved with dignity.

  • Designed for multi-generational legacy
  • Built around privacy and consent expectations
  • Structured for future premium and immersive experiences
Consent and Responsibility

Family participation must be handled carefully.

Tomb of Light’s public-facing policies are designed to make clear that living individuals should not be added casually, minors require parent or guardian permission, and inaccurate or disputed data must have a correction path.

Living family members

Family entries involving living individuals should be based on lawful, respectful participation and proper authorization where required.

  • Consent-sensitive onboarding
  • Correction and removal pathways
  • Identity-aware family handling

Minors and sensitive family data

If minors are ever included in a family experience, verified parent or guardian permission should be required. Sensitive family material should receive heightened protection.

  • Parental or guardian authorization expectations
  • Private-by-default treatment
  • Limited public exposure design
