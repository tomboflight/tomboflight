How Tomb of Light turns family history into a living digital legacy.

Tomb of Light guides families through a premium process that organizes lineage, structures portraits and relationships, protects private records, and delivers a digital experience designed to be explored across generations.

Core Process

The family legacy process in four clear stages.

Tomb of Light is designed as a guided journey from intake to final delivery, giving families a more structured and meaningful way to preserve lineage.

01

Begin with a Family Anchor

Every experience begins with a central portrait, person, memorial point, or family branch that serves as the anchor for the lineage structure.

02

Organize the Legacy Record

Names, portraits, stories, media, and relationships are structured into a guided family architecture rather than left scattered across folders and devices.

03

Build the Experience

The family structure is assembled into a visual lineage experience that can support branches, generations, and a more cinematic style of exploration.

04

Deliver and Maintain

Once complete, the family receives a guided delivery experience with options for maintenance, updates, and future lineage expansion over time.

The Foundation

Private family content is handled with care.

Tomb of Light is built around the principle that family memory should be preserved with dignity. Personal portraits, legacy records, stories, and private materials are meant to be handled in a controlled environment rather than exposed carelessly.

  • Family records are intended to remain private by design.
  • Controlled access matters when lineage includes living family members.
  • Legacy preservation should support continuity without sacrificing dignity.

Consent and Responsibility

Family participation must be handled carefully.

Tomb of Light is designed to support a respectful process for including living family members, handling sensitive records, and addressing corrections when needed.

Living Family Members

Entries involving living individuals should be handled through respectful participation, proper authorization, and a structure that supports correction pathways where necessary.

  • Consent-sensitive onboarding
  • Correction and update pathways
  • Respectful family handling

Minors and Sensitive Data

If minors or sensitive family materials are included, heightened care and appropriate parent or guardian authorization should apply.

  • Parent or guardian authorization expectations
  • Private-by-default treatment
  • Limited exposure design
