Trust Framework

Security built around privacy, stewardship, and responsible access.

Tomb of Light approaches security as a core part of digital legacy stewardship. The platform is designed to support secure website operations, careful account handling, private family record protection, and responsible operational controls over time.

Current Website Protections

Public website safeguards begin with disciplined fundamentals.

Tomb of Light is structured to support a modern public-facing website with careful transport protection, controlled deployment practices, and limited operational access.

Encrypted Connections

Website traffic should be served over encrypted connections so information exchanged between visitors and the site remains protected in transit.

  • HTTPS delivery expectations
  • Transport-layer protection in transit
  • Reduced interception risk

Managed Hosting Controls

Tomb of Light is intended to rely on managed infrastructure and controlled deployment practices appropriate for a modern premium platform.

  • Protected deployment workflow
  • Environment-aware handling
  • Operational access kept limited

Operational Discipline

Security is strengthened by limiting unnecessary exposure, maintaining clear workflows, and avoiding casual handling of sensitive systems and credentials.

  • Restricted administrative handling
  • Controlled operational processes
  • Ongoing review mindset
Platform Security Direction

Private family content should be handled separately from public-facing layers.

Tomb of Light is built around the principle that private family history deserves careful treatment. Sensitive portraits, stories, records, and identity-rich materials should remain protected in controlled systems rather than being casually exposed through public mechanisms.

  • Private family records should remain protected by design.
  • Public-facing layers should avoid unnecessary sensitive exposure.
  • Security decisions should support dignity, privacy, and continuity.
Account Protection

Access should be limited, intentional, and role-aware.

Security is not just about infrastructure. It also depends on how access is granted, how credentials are handled, and how administrative responsibility is controlled.

01

Access Control

Administrative and operational access should be limited to authorized personnel with a real and legitimate business purpose.

02

Credential Handling

Sensitive account credentials should be handled carefully and never treated as casual, shareable information within normal operations.

03

Future Hardening

As the platform matures, stronger authentication, role-based controls, and additional verification measures may be layered in over time.

Responsible Disclosure

Report a potential security issue responsibly and directly.

If you believe you have identified a security issue affecting the public website, Tomb of Light asks that you report it responsibly and avoid any attempt to access, alter, expose, or misuse private information or platform systems.

What to Include

  • A clear description of the issue
  • The page, feature, or workflow affected
  • Steps to reproduce, if known
  • Relevant screenshots or technical context

Expected Conduct

  • Do not access data you are not authorized to view
  • Do not disrupt services or availability
  • Do not attempt destructive testing
  • Do not expose private information publicly

How to Contact

Security-related communications should be directed through Tomb of Light’s official support or compliance contact channels, with enough detail provided to support responsible review.

Limits and Evolving Controls

Security is an ongoing discipline, not a one-time claim.

No platform can promise absolute security. Tomb of Light intends to continue improving technical safeguards, operational practices, and protection measures as the platform evolves.

Continuous Improvement

Security controls may change as the platform grows and operational maturity increases.

Access Maturity

Additional identity and access safeguards may be added as future workflows expand.

Policy Updates

Public-facing pages may be updated over time to reflect security posture improvements.

Stewardship Mindset

The long-term goal is durable protection aligned with privacy, trust, and family dignity.
