Security and infrastructure

Security at Tomb of Light

Tomb of Light is being built with a privacy-first security direction focused on protecting family legacy information, limiting public exposure of sensitive records, and strengthening trust through controlled access, policy discipline, and responsible operational safeguards.

This page describes Tomb of Light’s current public-facing security direction. Some controls described here may be in staged development, planned rollout, or operational hardening phases as the platform evolves.
Core approach

Private family data should not be treated like public blockchain content.

Tomb of Light is being designed so that family portraits, stories, records, and identity-sensitive information remain protected in controlled systems, while any future blockchain layer is limited to ownership or reference functions that avoid unnecessary exposure of personal family data.

Private-by-default design

Sensitive family content is intended to remain off-chain and under controlled platform protection rather than being published openly.

  • Off-chain protection model
  • Limited public metadata exposure
  • Separation between ownership and content

Access-aware systems

Family legacy material should be available only through appropriate permissions, account protection, and role-based handling.

  • Controlled account access
  • Future role and permission layers
  • Restricted handling of sensitive records

Security-minded operations

Operational direction includes secure hosting, transport protection, monitoring, and practical response processes as the company grows.

  • Infrastructure hardening direction
  • Monitoring and review goals
  • Incident response planning
Data protection

Encryption and protected storage direction

Tomb of Light’s platform direction includes protecting data both in transit and at rest using modern security practices appropriate to the sensitivity of family legacy information. Family content is intended to be stored in controlled systems rather than embedded directly in public blockchain records.

  • Transport protection for data moving between users and platform services
  • Protected storage direction for sensitive family media and records
  • Separation between private vault content and public ownership references
Account protection

Identity and access safeguards

Tomb of Light expects account security to be a major part of trust. As platform capabilities mature, access control direction includes secure authentication flows, stronger admin protection, and limited access based on user role or family relationship context.

  • Password-based authentication with future stronger options
  • Multi-factor authentication direction for staff or privileged access
  • Role-based or permission-aware handling for sensitive operations
Infrastructure

Hosting, continuity, and resilience

Secure platform operation depends on infrastructure choices as much as page design. Tomb of Light’s operational direction includes secure hosting, HTTPS enforcement, service hardening, backup planning, and resilience measures intended to reduce risk and support continuity.

  • HTTPS-first service delivery
  • Production hardening and controlled deployment practices
  • Backup and recovery planning for critical systems and records
Monitoring

Logging, review, and incident awareness

Security depends on the ability to detect and investigate problems. Tomb of Light’s long-term direction includes service monitoring, operational logging, and review pathways for security-relevant events involving accounts, access, or protected records.

  • Operational event logging direction
  • Review pathways for suspicious activity
  • Escalation planning for platform incidents
Responsible handling

Security also includes consent, sensitivity, and family dignity.

Because Tomb of Light deals with family identity, memory, and lineage, security is not only a technical issue. It is also about respectful participation, careful handling of disputes, and limiting misuse of sensitive family information.

Consent-sensitive participation

Living individuals should not be included carelessly. Sensitive family inclusion decisions should be handled with respect, lawful basis where required, and clear correction or removal pathways.

  • Respect for living family members
  • Correction and removal processes
  • Heightened care for sensitive family disputes

Minors and protected family material

Where minors or especially sensitive legacy content are involved, parental or guardian permission and private-by-default treatment are expected as part of the platform’s public trust direction.

  • Parental or guardian authorization expectations
  • Protected handling of minor-related content
  • Higher sensitivity for vulnerable family records
Incident response

Response direction for security issues

Tomb of Light intends to review credible security issues promptly and take appropriate steps based on the nature of the issue, the systems involved, and the potential impact on users or protected family material.

  1. Review reported security concerns and validate whether the issue is credible.
  2. Assess scope, severity, and whether protected user or family data may be affected.
  3. Contain, remediate, and document the issue based on operational needs.
  4. Improve controls, procedures, or infrastructure where needed.
Responsible disclosure

How to report a security concern

If you believe you have found a security issue affecting Tomb of Light, please report it responsibly and in good faith. Do not attempt to access data that does not belong to you, disrupt services, or expose private information publicly.

  • Provide a clear description of the issue
  • Include the affected page, feature, or workflow
  • Share reproduction details where possible
  • Do not exploit, exfiltrate, or publish sensitive data

Security contact: admin@tomboflight.com
