Security

Tomb of Light is being developed with a privacy-first mindset and a security-conscious approach to website operations, account protection, and long-term stewardship of family legacy systems.

Request Access Privacy Policy
Current Website Protections

Encrypted connections

The public website is intended to be served over encrypted connections so information exchanged between a visitor’s browser and the site is protected in transit.

  • HTTPS delivery expectations
  • Basic transport-layer protection
  • Reduced interception risk in transit

Hosting and infrastructure controls

Tomb of Light is intended to rely on managed hosting and platform protections appropriate for a modern public-facing website, including controlled deployment and environment handling.

  • Protected deployment workflow
  • Environment separation where applicable
  • Operational access kept limited
Platform Direction

Private family content should not be exposed as public blockchain data.

The long-term platform direction is to separate sensitive family stories, media, records, and identity-rich material from any public ownership or reference layer. This means private legacy content should remain protected in controlled systems rather than placed openly on a public chain.

  • Private family data is intended to remain off-chain.
  • Public ownership layers should minimize sensitive exposure.
  • Security design should support dignity, privacy, and continuity.
Account Protection
1

Access control

Internal access to website or platform administration should be limited to authorized personnel with a legitimate operational purpose.

2

Credential handling

Tomb of Light should avoid exposing passwords or sensitive account credentials in readable form and should use modern authentication practices where available.

3

Future authentication hardening

As the platform matures, additional account protections such as stronger verification, role-based access, and multi-factor authentication may be introduced.

Responsible Disclosure

Report a potential security issue responsibly.

If you believe you have identified a security issue affecting the public website, contact Tomb of Light directly and provide enough detail for review. Please do not attempt to access, alter, or expose private information, disrupt services, or test beyond what is legally and ethically permitted.

What to include

  • A clear description of the issue
  • The page or feature affected
  • Steps to reproduce, if known
  • Relevant screenshots or technical context

How to contact

Send security-related reports to the contact channel listed for Tomb of Light support or compliance communications. Reports should be reviewed in a reasonable timeframe based on severity and available operational capacity.

Limits and Evolving Controls

Security is an ongoing process.

No system can promise absolute security. Tomb of Light intends to continue improving technical safeguards, operational processes, and user protection measures as the platform evolves.

  • Controls may change as the platform matures.
  • Additional identity and access safeguards may be added over time.
  • Public pages may be updated to reflect improvements in security posture.
