Trust & Protection

Security

Effective Date: March 11, 2026
Last Updated: March 11, 2026

Tomb of Light is being built with security, privacy, and responsible data handling as core operating principles. Because the platform is designed to preserve sensitive family legacy information, we take the protection of accounts, records, and submitted content seriously.

This page describes our current security posture, development direction, and how security concerns can be reported to us.

1. Security Commitment

Tomb of Light is designed with security as a core principle. Our platform uses encrypted connections, secure authentication practices, and privacy-first architecture to help protect family legacy data, account activity, and submitted records.

Sensitive personal information is not intended to be written directly to public blockchain networks. Where blockchain-related ownership features are developed in the future, those systems are intended to remain separate from private family content and sensitive records.

2. Current Security Measures

Our website and evolving platform may use measures such as:

  • HTTPS-encrypted connections for website traffic
  • Access controls for account-related functions and administrative operations
  • Hosting-level protections and infrastructure monitoring
  • Secure handling of support, request, and account-related submissions
  • Limited internal access to personal information based on operational need
  • Ongoing review of privacy, consent, and data exposure risks

3. Authentication and Account Protection

Users are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of their account credentials and for protecting access to their devices and email accounts. If you believe your account has been accessed without authorization, contact us immediately.

Strong passwords should be used for all Tomb of Light accounts. Two-factor authentication may be introduced in a future platform update to strengthen account protection.

4. Privacy-First Architecture

Tomb of Light is being developed so that private family details, sensitive records, and legacy narratives can remain under controlled platform management instead of being placed openly on public blockchain systems.

This separation is an important part of our security and privacy design approach. The goal is to reduce unnecessary public exposure of personal family information while still allowing future digital ownership structures where appropriate.

5. Security Limitations

No website, server environment, application, or data transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Although we use safeguards designed to reduce risk, we cannot guarantee absolute protection against all threats, attacks, failures, or unauthorized actions.

Users should also take reasonable steps to protect their own accounts, devices, networks, and email systems.

6. Responsible Disclosure

If you discover a security issue, vulnerability, or suspected weakness involving Tomb of Light, please report it to us responsibly so we can investigate.

Please include:

  • A clear description of the issue
  • The affected page, feature, or workflow
  • Steps to reproduce the issue, if known
  • Screenshots or supporting details, if available
  • Your contact information for follow-up, if you want a response

Please do not exploit vulnerabilities, access data you are not authorized to access, disrupt services, or test the site in a way that could harm users, infrastructure, or data.

7. Security Updates and Platform Evolution

Tomb of Light is an evolving platform. Our security controls, authentication features, infrastructure design, and internal procedures may change over time as the platform grows.

We may update this page as security practices mature, new features are introduced, or risk management standards are revised.

8. Related Policies

Additional information about how we handle data and user rights can be found in our Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and Data Request pages.
