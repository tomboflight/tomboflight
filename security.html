Important Note

This page is a general summary, not a complete technical specification.

Tomb of Light uses administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to reduce risk, protect information, and support secure operation of the public website and platform-facing workflows. Specific controls, configurations, and vendors may change over time and are not fully described on this page.

Website and Platform Protections

Public-facing protections begin with disciplined fundamentals.

Encrypted Website Sessions

Tomb of Light expects public website traffic to be delivered over encrypted connections to help protect information in transit.

Controlled Administrative Access

Access to internal systems and platform-management functions is intended to be limited to authorized personnel or service providers with a legitimate business purpose.

Security Review Mindset

Tomb of Light continues to review workflows, system exposure, and operating practices to reduce unnecessary risk and strengthen platform stewardship over time.

Private Family Records

Private family content is intended for controlled systems, not casual public exposure.

Tomb of Light is designed around the principle that private family history, portraits, lineage records, and related materials deserve careful handling. Private family content is not intended for public display by default and may be handled through protected workflows, access controls, and account-based experiences where applicable.

Account Protection

Access depends on user behavior as well as platform controls.

01

Password Discipline

Users should choose a strong, unique password and keep credentials confidential.

02

Access Limitation

Administrative and operational access should be limited to people with a legitimate business need.

03

Workflow Review

Tomb of Light may review suspicious activity, misuse reports, and workflow anomalies to protect users and platform integrity.

Uploads and Sensitive Materials

Users should submit only what is reasonably necessary for the stated purpose.

Verification and family-record workflows may involve sensitive materials. Tomb of Light asks users not to upload Social Security cards, full Social Security numbers, payment card information, or other unnecessary sensitive information unless specifically requested for a defined workflow.

Responsible Disclosure

Report a potential security issue responsibly and directly.

If you believe you have identified a security issue affecting the public website or a Tomb of Light workflow, report it responsibly and do not access, alter, expose, or misuse information or systems you are not authorized to use.

What to Include

  • A clear description of the issue
  • The page, feature, or workflow affected
  • Steps to reproduce, if known
  • Relevant screenshots or technical context

Expected Conduct

  • Do not access data you are not authorized to view
  • Do not disrupt services or availability
  • Do not perform destructive testing
  • Do not expose private information publicly

How to Contact

Send security-related reports to support@tomboflight.com with the subject line “Security Report.”

Limits

No system can promise absolute security.

Security is an ongoing discipline, not a one-time claim. No website, platform, storage system, transmission method, or account system can be guaranteed to be absolutely secure at all times.
